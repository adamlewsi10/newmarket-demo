How HubSpot can unify your customer data, power personalised marketing, and support Newmarket's ambitious growth — starting now, not when the back-office transformation is complete.

The Client

Newmarket Holidays

Founded in 1983, Newmarket Holidays has grown into one of the UK's largest independently owned escorted tour operators, serving approximately 48,000 passengers per year and generating £85 million in annual revenue. Based in Wallington, Surrey, with around 190 employees, the company has won more than 100 awards — including Best Escorted Tour Operator at the British Travel Awards for 13 consecutive years.

Now PE-backed by Soho Square Capital, Newmarket is entering a pivotal phase of strategic technology transformation. The "Flex" project represents the company's ambition to move beyond rigid, structured escorted tours and offer pre- and post-tour extensions — stopovers, flights, hotels, and experiences — that increase Average Order Value and capture revenue currently lost to more flexible competitors.

This CRM transformation sits alongside a broader technology programme encompassing the Central Reservations System (CRS) and Destination Management System (DMS), with a target go-live of September 2027. The CRM, however, can deliver immediate value by implementing first — integrating with the existing Tiger Bay system while the back-office replacement proceeds in parallel.

The Challenge

What's Holding Newmarket Back

01

No Single Customer View

Customer interactions are fragmented across Tiger Bay's booking system and manual workarounds. There is no unified record that captures bookings, enquiries, marketing engagement, complaints, and preferences in one place.

02

Rigid Back-Office System

Tiger Bay cannot support the "Flex" model — pre- and post-tour extensions that customers increasingly expect. A marketing director's own family recently booked with a competitor because the system couldn't handle a simple pre-tour stopover.

03

Manual Marketing & Comms

Without modern automation, customer communications rely on manual processes. Flight change notifications, upsell campaigns, and post-booking engagement are difficult to execute at scale — particularly with AI-powered personalisation.

04

Siloed Operational Workflows

Customer Operations relies on Tiger Bay "categories" for task queues such as cancellations and amendments. These lack the flexibility of modern workflow engines, creating bottlenecks and manual handoffs across teams.
